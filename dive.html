Introduction

Water quality is vital for various sectors, including industries, public health, ecosystems, and the environment. This study scrutinizes a specific water source's quality and regulatory adherence, pivotal for drinking, farming, recreation, and industry. Despite existing control mechanisms, persistent challenges stem from pollution and natural variations. Economic and societal development has led to significant water pollution, surpassing ecosystems' self-purification capacity. Technological advancements in sensors revolutionize water quality monitoring, offering real-time data on parameters like pH, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, temperature, and contaminants, empowering decision-makers to protect human health and contribute to broader efforts in sustainable water resource management.

Components

Arduino Uno

The Arduino Uno, a widely-used open-source microcontroller board, is powered by the ATmega328P microcontroller, running at 16 MHz. With 14 digital I/O pins, 6 PWM outputs, and 6 analog inputs, it facilitates diverse interfacing. Featuring a USB interface for programming and communication, the board can be powered via USB or an external supply, making it a versatile choice for DIY electronics projects.

ESP-32

The ESP32 is a versatile and powerful microcontroller and Wi-Fi module widely used in IoT applications. It features dual-core processors, Bluetooth connectivity, and a range of GPIO pins. The ESP32 is known for its efficiency, low power consumption, and compatibility with Arduino IDE, making it a popular choice for embedded systems and IoT projects.

pH Sensor

A pH sensor is a device that measures the acidity or alkalinity of a solution, indicating its pH level. It is commonly used in various applications, such as water quality monitoring, agriculture, and scientific research. The sensor typically provides real-time data on the pH of a liquid, helping users assess and control the acidity or alkalinity of a substance.

Turbidity Sensor

Turbidity refers to the cloudiness or haziness of a fluid caused by the presence of suspended particles. It is a measure of how much light is scattered or absorbed by these particles in the water. Turbidity is commonly used as an indicator of water quality, with higher turbidity levels suggesting a higher concentration of suspended solids in the liquid. Monitoring turbidity is crucial in assessing the clarity and potential impurities in water for various applications, including environmental monitoring and water treatment.

DS18B20 Temperature Sensor

The DS18B20 is a digital temperature sensor known for its accuracy and simplicity. It operates on the one-wire protocol, allowing multiple sensors to be connected to a single digital pin. Widely used in electronics and IoT projects, the DS18B20 provides precise temperature measurements and is often integrated into systems for monitoring and control applications.

Voltage Shifter

A voltage shifter is a device that changes the voltage level of an electrical signal from one level to another. It is commonly used in electronics to interface components or devices that operate at different voltage levels. Voltage shifters ensure compatibility and proper communication between components with varying voltage requirements, preventing potential damage and ensuring smooth operation in mixed-voltage systems.
